A little about myself.

I’m Collin, currently a senior studying Human-Centered Design & Engineering and Informatics at the University of Washington. Seeking UX and product design positions upon June 2020 graduation!

My friends will tell you that I can be broken down into three pillars: film, cars, and entrepreneurship, all of which being the most significant contributors to my perspective on problem solving and design thinking today.

  • Film. I love movies. I could spend all day discussing Christopher Nolan's non-linear storytelling or David Fincher's bizarre color palettes. Favorite flick? Gotta be Pulp Fiction.
    • I also direct and edit my own projects sometimes; viewable here.
  • Cars. My very first design projects actually started in the garage, fabricating completely custom engine and body kits to cars they were never intended for.
    • My most intense project to date: Boosted S2000.
  • Entrepreneurship. When I was 15, I discovered the wonderland that is Craigslist.org (and soon after, OfferUp!) to make some money on the side selling my restored automotive projects. Since then, I've made thousands of connections, meeting new people as a seller and as a buyer, and having the opportunity to understand their needs and wants from a unique angle. I've gained invaluable skills from these experiences throughout the years – public speaking, sales, and negotiation, just to name a few.

If you're interested in hearing more about any one of my projects, please feel free to contact me.


RESUME

The logo.

I took an elective called Engineering Drafting and Computer-Aided Design, which partly consisted of recreating isometric views of various mechanical parts, as a mechanical engineer might draft. This course served as exposure to designing real manufactured parts, and we were scrutinized over the precision of line lengths, arc radii, and grid spacing. Needless to say, this course improved my attention to detail tremendously.

That same quarter, my Visual Communications course discussed artistic perspective, and stressed the importance of breaking from traditional, logical constraints, and force more abstract points of view.

A few dozen sketches later and I found the intersection point — a personal logo that represents how these two very polarizing schools of thought shaped my design thinking.

Made with HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap.
